7. Privacy Protection Guidance

The Province takes your privacy seriously. Although the Service includes security features, you must also take steps to protect your privacy:

  1. Do not set up an account for the Service if you cannot fully control who has access to your device. If you share your phone or password with others, the security of your information is at risk. Anyone who has access to your device may be able to open your account and see your:
    • prescriptions, such as birth control medication,
    • health visit and procedure details,
    • vaccination status, and
    • blood test results, including sexually transmitted infection testing.
  2. If you can't use the Service because you don't have your own secure device, we can tell you about other ways to access your Health Information. Email us at: HealthGateway@gov.bc.ca
  3. Before setting up an account, make sure you have a password on your device that only you know. For tips on how to create a secure password, visit https://www.getcybersafe.gc.ca/en/secure-your-accounts/passphrases-passwords-and-pins. Alternatively, you can secure your device with facial recognition or fingerprint identification.
  4. Make sure to log out of your account and close the screen once you are finished using the Service. Lock and store your device securely when not in use.

For more tips on how to protect your privacy, visit: https://www.getcybersafe.gc.ca.

If you notice anything unusual in your account or if you have any privacy questions, contact us for help: HealthGateway@gov.bc.ca
